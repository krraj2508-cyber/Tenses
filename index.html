Golden Rules for Tenses

3 Steps to Solve Any Grammar Question

1 Check the "Signal Words"

The sentence usually tells you the time through specific adverbs. Look for these words first:

If you see... Use this Tense
Daily, Always, Usually, Often Simple Present (V1 s/es)
Yesterday, Last week, Ago, In 1947 Simple Past (V2)
Tomorrow, Next year, Soon Simple Future (will + V1)
Now, At this moment, Look! Present Continuous (is/am/are + ing)
Just, Already, Yet, Recently Present Perfect (has/have + V3)
2 Check the Subject Number

Ensure the verb matches the subject (Subject-Verb Agreement).

  • He, She, It, Ram (Singular): Likes 's' or 'es' (e.g., goes, plays, has, is).
  • I, We, You, They (Plural): Do not add 's' to the main verb (e.g., go, play, have, are).
Exception Rule: 'I' is singular but takes 'have' and 'do' (not has/does).
3 Since vs. For

Used in Perfect Continuous Tenses (has been / had been).

Word Rule Example
Since Point of time (Start date) Since morning, Since 1990
For Period of time (Duration) For 2 hours, For 5 years
Teacher's Note: Always identify the time signal first.

Advanced Tense Rules

4 The "No -ing" Rule (Stative Verbs)

Some verbs describe a state, not an action. Never use these in Continuous (ing) forms.

Verb Type Incorrect ❌ Correct ✅
Senses (See, Hear, Smell) I am hearing a noise. I hear a noise.
Emotion (Love, Hate, Want) I am wanting a pen. I want a pen.
Mental (Know, Understand) I am understanding you. I understand you.
Possession (Have, Own) He is having a car. He has a car.
5 The "Before & After" Logic

When two things happened in the past, use Past Perfect for the first action and Simple Past for the second.

Formula: 1st Action (Had + V3) ... 2nd Action (V2)
  • The patient had died (1st) before the doctor arrived (2nd).
  • When I reached the station (2nd), the train had left (1st).
6 The "If" Conditionals

If the sentence starts with "If", the tenses must balance correctly.

Condition (If...) Result Clause
Type 1: If + Present (V1)
If he runs fast...		 Will + V1
...he will win.
Type 2: If + Past (V2)
If he ran fast...		 Would + V1
...he would win.
Type 3: If + Had + V3
If he had run fast...		 Would Have + V3
...he would have won.
Teacher's Note: Stative verbs are frequently asked in error correction.

Master Exceptions

The "Exam Traps" to Watch Out For

Trap #1 The "Since" Clause Rule
When "Since" is used to join two sentences, the verb after "since" is always Simple Past (V2), and the main verb is Present Perfect.
❌ Two years have passed since he has come here.
✅ Two years have passed since he came here.
Trap #2 The "It is time" Rule
If a sentence starts with "It is time" or "It is high time," the verb that follows must be in the Simple Past (V2), even though it refers to now.
❌ It is time we start the class.
✅ It is time we started the class.
Nuance Will vs. Going To
Use "Going to" for plans you have already made. Use "Will" for instant decisions made at the moment of speaking.

Scenario: You bought a ticket yesterday.

✅ "I am going to fly to Delhi." (Plan)

Scenario: The phone is ringing.

✅ "I will answer it." (Instant Decision)
Usage Used To vs. Would
For past habits that are now finished, use "Used to".
❌ I was smoking before.
✅ I used to smoke.
Teacher's Note: Trap #1 and #2 are very common in Board Exams.

Mastery Rules

Advanced Moods & Sentence Transformations

11
The "Unreal Past" (Subjunctive)

When we talk about imaginary situations or wishes, we use the Past Tense to talk about the Present.

The "I Wish" Logic:
✅ "I wish I knew the answer."
(Meaning: I don't know it right now. We use V2 'knew' for present regret.)
✅ "I wish I had met him yesterday."
(Meaning: I didn't meet him. We use Past Perfect 'had met' for past regret.)

Exception:

Always use "were" instead of "was" for imaginary status.

✅ "If I were a bird, I would fly." (Not 'was')
12
Inversion (Hardly / Scarcely)

When a sentence starts with negative adverbs like Hardly, Scarcely, or No Sooner, the helping verb must come before the subject.

The Formula:
Hardly + had + Subject + V3 ... when ...
✅ "Hardly had I reached the station when the train left."
Note: It is NOT "Hardly I had reached". The 'had' jumps forward.

Alternative Formula:
No sooner + did + Subject + V1 ... than ...
✅ "No sooner did he see the police than he ran."
13
The "By The Time" Rule

This phrase acts as a deadline. It forces the use of Perfect Tenses.

Scenario A: Past Deadline
✅ "By the time we arrived, the movie had started."
Rule: Simple Past (arrived) + Past Perfect (had started).

Scenario B: Future Deadline
✅ "By the time you return, I will have finished the work."
Rule: Simple Present (return) + Future Perfect (will have finished).
Teacher's Note: Rule 12 is extremely common in "Do as Directed" questions.

Why Tenses Matter

Tenses are the heartbeat of a sentence. Without them, we cannot distinguish history from the future, or a habit from a single moment. By mastering these 12 rules, you have gained the power to tell stories with precision and clarity.

Final Mastery Checklist:

✔ I can identify the 3 main time zones (Past, Present, Future).
✔ I understand the difference between "Simple" and "Continuous."
✔ I know to look for "Signal Words" (yesterday, now, since) before answering.
✔ I can spot the "Exam Traps" like Stative Verbs and Conditionals.

"Practice is the key. Keep observing these rules in your daily reading."
